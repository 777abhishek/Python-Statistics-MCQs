📊 Topic 3
Central Tendency — FormulasQ9–Q12
Q9Medium
Which measure is used for categorical (nominal) data?
💡 ExplanationMode is the only meaningful central tendency for categorical data (blood group, gender, language).
Q10Hard
Group A: n=3, mean=50. Group B: n=7, mean=80. Combined mean = ?
💡 ExplanationCombined = (3×50 + 7×80)/(3+7) = (150+560)/10 = 710/10 = 71.0.
Q11Medium
Median of [15, 25, 35, 45, 55, 65] is:
💡 Explanationn=6. Middle two = 35 and 45. Median = (35+45)/2 = 40.
Q12Tricky
When is geometric mean preferred over arithmetic mean?
💡 ExplanationGeometric mean = ⁿ√(x₁×x₂×...×xₙ). Best for multiplicative data: growth rates, investment returns, ratios.
📏 Topic 4
Dispersion — Range & Std DevQ13–Q16
Q13Medium
Range = Max − Min. For [3, 7, 2, 15, 9], Range = ?
💡 ExplanationMax=15, Min=2. Range = 15-2 = 13.
Q14Hard
Why is range a poor measure of dispersion?
💡 ExplanationRange only uses max and min — one outlier completely changes it, ignoring all other data points.
Q15Medium
Coefficient of Variation (CV) = 15%. This means:
💡 ExplanationCV = (Std/Mean)×100. CV=15% means the std is 15% of the mean — a measure of relative variability.
Q16Tricky
Population std of [5,5,5,5,5] is:
💡 ExplanationAll values are identical → no deviation from mean → std = 0.
