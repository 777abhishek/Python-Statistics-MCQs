🎲 Topic 7
Probability — ComprehensiveQ25–Q28
Q25Hard
P(A∪B) when A and B are independent, P(A)=0.4, P(B)=0.3:
💡 ExplanationP(A∪B)=P(A)+P(B)−P(A∩B). For independent: P(A∩B)=0.4×0.3=0.12. P(A∪B)=0.4+0.3−0.12=0.58.
Q26Medium
Classical probability is used for:
💡 ExplanationClassical probability: theoretical scenarios with equally likely outcomes — rolling fair dice, drawing from shuffled cards.
Q27Tricky
Disjoint (mutually exclusive) events vs Independent events:
💡 ExplanationDisjoint: cannot co-occur (P(A∩B)=0). Independent: P(A∩B)=P(A)×P(B). Disjoint events are DEPENDENT (if A occurs, B cannot).
Q28Medium
If P(rain today)=0.3, P(no rain today) = ?
💡 ExplanationComplement rule: P(no rain) = 1 − P(rain) = 1 − 0.3 = 0.7.
